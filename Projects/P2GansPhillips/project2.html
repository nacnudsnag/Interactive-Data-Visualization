Hexbin Forest Fire Visualization:

This map gives us an idea of a single fire centric view of forest fires. The idea is to see how forest fires change over time, and how they clump together. This also allows us to see how long a forest fire lasts. Using this map, we can tell that forest fires aren't as permanent or consistent as we might think they are. On any given day, the areas that there are forest fires changes dramatically. Furthermore, it shows that although there are certainly clumps in the california area, there are also clumps of forest fires in the southeast as well as the dakota region. It also demonstrates that areas where there are larger clumps of forest fires also typically have a few big fires that are in the center of the clumpings. This is different than the more sporadic fires. Typically the more sporadic fires are smaller and don't last as long.
