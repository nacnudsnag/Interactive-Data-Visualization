The Data Set: Forest Fires

The data that I am looking at is information about a national park in portugal specifically related to forest fires. The data is geographic, and details various attributes that are related to forest fires such as the temperature, the precipitation, soil information, and most importantly, the amount an area has been burned. Many use this dataset to predict what causes forest fires, and to predict where future forest fires will take place. The actual attributes detailed are.
  • X Coordinate: The location's x coordinate within the park
  • Y Coordinate: The location's y coordinate within the park
  • Month: The Month, January through December
  • Day: Day of Week, Monday-Sunday
  • FFMC: Fine Fuel Moisture Code: a numerical rating of the moisture content of litter and other cured fine fuels
  • DMC: Duff Moisture Code: the moisture content of loosely-compacted organic layers of moderate depth.
  • DC: Drought Code: indicator of moisture, content in deep, compact organic layers
  • ISI: Initial Spread Index: combines the FFMC and wind speed to indicate the expected rate of fire spread
  • Temp: Temperature in degrees Celsius
  • RH: Relative Humidity of the specific area in %
  • Wind: Wind Speed in Km/Hour
  • Rain: The rain in the given area
  • Area: The amount of area burned in the given location (in Hectares)
